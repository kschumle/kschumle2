Keep in mind that these are the raw (ungraded) prices for good condition cards. Some older cards graded highly can go for tens of thousands of dollars. Never get into card collecting to try and make money though. Prices are always volatile and it is all random at the end of the day.

Yu-Gi-Oh

Konami likes to reprint cards constantly to make sure players have access to the cards they want to play, so Yu-Gi-Oh cards do not generally hold as much value as the other games. They have recently begun releasing special serialized cards that do reach crazy levels, but for the most part they are affordable. This means it is much cheaper to complete your Yu-Gi-Oh collections than some of the others! This table shows some recent high-value Yu-Gi-Oh cards.

Other

The values in the other TCGs can vary widely depending on the game's popularity, the cards rarity, and in the case of a lot of them, whether it is stamped with a signature or not. I have included some examples of some different high-value cards from different TCGs in the table here. Keep in mind that these cards can be ludicrously difficult to pull from any given pack, but there is a lot of value even in some of the lesser-known TCGs. Once again though, never collect strictly for value as you will always lose money on TCGs.
